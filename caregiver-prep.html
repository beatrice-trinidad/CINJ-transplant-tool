Caregiver Preparation

Preparing for Transplant

While you undergo treatment, you might not be able to manage your medical and household affairs on your own for several weeks or months. A full-time family caregiver is needed to ensure that you promptly get the medical attention you need, and that your household is a safe environment. Before you comes home from the hospital, there are a few things your caregiver should do to prepare yourself and your home.

Clean your home thoroughly.

The patient's immune system will be compromised and they must stay in a germ-free environment. Make sure you get rid of all dust and mildew before the patient arrives.

Organize your support system.

Caring for a transplant patient can be a lot of work, and caregivers must devote a lot of time and energy to care fot he patient in the weeks or months that they recover. This can be a lot for one person, and it can help to involve multiple caregivers to take on some of the responsibility.

Meet with a social worker.

Speaking with a social worker can be very helpful to manage both the practical demands of a caregiver and the emotional demands as well. Meet with the social worker as soon as you can to better prepare for the post-transplant period, and feel free to reach out in the future.
